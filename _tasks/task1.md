# The Jigsaw Puzzle

During a covert operation in Sherlock Holmes' private archives, a crucial photographic record was sabotaged. The image was broken into nine equal tiles.
All the nine photographic tiles survived. But before they could be secured, an unknown adversary tampered with them:
Each plate was rotated by a distinct mechanical distortion.
All nine tiles were then scrambled and reassembled into a misleading composite.
Holmes identified a subtle but decisive clue: the top-left pixel of every tile. This pixel is a compact encoding of the tile's original position and orientation.

### The Encoded Information

The top-left pixel (row=0, col=0) of each tile encodes two values in its BGR channels:

| Channel | Value to be extracted | What the value encodes |
|---------|---|---|
| Red | LSB | Original tile index (0–8) |
| Green | MSB | Clockwise rotation that was applied to the original tile (0–3) |

<div class="rotation-codes">
  <h4>Rotation codes:</h4>
  <ul>
    <li>0 → 0°</li>
    <li>1 → 90° CW</li>
    <li>2 → 180°</li>
    <li>3 → 270° CW</li>
  </ul>
</div>
